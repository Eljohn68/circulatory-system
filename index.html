By: A Future Medical Pro 💉

📌 What is the Circulatory System?

The circulatory system is a complex network responsible for transporting blood, oxygen, nutrients, and hormones throughout the body. It is crucial for survival and plays a key role in maintaining homeostasis.

"Your heart beats about 100,000 times a day. It deserves a little attention!" 💓

🧩 Key Functions:

  • Delivers oxygen to cells
  • Removes carbon dioxide
  • Transports nutrients and hormones
  • Protects against disease
  • Regulates body temperature

🧠 Parts of the Circulatory System

  1. Heart – The main pump that keeps blood moving
  2. Arteries – Carry oxygenated blood away from the heart
  3. Veins – Bring deoxygenated blood back to the heart
  4. Capillaries – Tiny blood vessels where exchange happens
  5. Blood – The fluid that carries oxygen, nutrients, and waste

💓 Main Pulse Points

Radial Pulse Wrist Use two fingers on the thumb side of wrist
Carotid Pulse Neck Press gently beside the windpipe
Brachial Pulse Inner elbow Useful for checking infant pulse
Femoral Pulse Groin Deep in the upper thigh area
Pedal Pulse Top of the foot Press lightly above the arch

❤️ Types of Heart Pulses

1. Resting Heart Rate

The number of beats per minute while at rest. Normal: 60–100 bpm (adults)

2. Maximum Heart Rate

Highest safe number of beats during exercise. Estimate: 220 - age

3. Target Heart Rate

Ideal heart rate zone for exercise, usually 50–85% of max rate

4. Irregular Pulse

May indicate arrhythmia or heart issues. Always consult a doctor.
